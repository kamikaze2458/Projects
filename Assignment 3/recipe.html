Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi soup is a rich and flavorful West African dish, especially popular in Nigeria. It is made primarily from ground melon seeds (called "egusi"), which give the soup its distinctive thick texture and nutty taste. The soup is typically cooked with palm oil, leafy vegetables like bitterleaf or spinach, and a variety of proteins such as beef, goat meat, fish, or stockfish. Seasonings like crayfish, pepper, and traditional spices enhance its depth of flavor. Egusi soup is often enjoyed with pounded yam, fufu, or eba, making it a hearty and satisfying meal that reflects the vibrant culinary heritage of the region.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: The preparation time for this delicacy is approximately 15 (fifteen) minutes.
Cooking time: The time spent in cooking this delicacy can range up to 30 to 40 minutes.
Number of Servings: This recipe serves about 4 to 6 people, depending on portion sizes.
Difficulty Level: Beginner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of ground egusi seeds
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cups of water or stock
  • 2 cups of leafy vegetables (bitterleaf, spinach, or ugu)
  • 1-2 tablespoons of ground crayfish
  • 1-2 tablespoons of ground pepper (to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, fish, or stockfish)

Instructions

  • In a large pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat until it is hot but not smoking.
  • Add the chopped onions and sauté until they are translucent.
  • Stir in the ground egusi seeds and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent burning.
  • Add the water or stock gradually, stirring to combine until you achieve a thick consistency.
  • Incorporate the assorted meats and allow them to cook through, about 10-15 minutes.
  • Add the leafy vegetables, ground crayfish, ground pepper, and salt. Stir well and let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  • Adjust seasoning as needed and serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba.

    • Tips

    • For a richer flavor, you can add locust beans (iru) or smoked fish.
    • Ensure the palm oil is well heated before adding the egusi to enhance its flavor.
    • Adjust the thickness of the soup by adding more water or stock as desired.
    • Feel free to customize the protein ingredients based on your preference.
    • Choose vegetables based on desired taste—bitterleaf for traditional, spinach for mild.
    • Store leftovers in the fridge (3–4 days) or freezer for longer shelf life.
